Introduction

The development of AI has always begotten the question of whether such an entity has the capability to replace human effort in various fields. The essay “AI should augment human intelligence, not replace it” presented by David De Cremer and Garry Kasparov discusses such questions through an interesting and knowledgeable set of eyes. Our group has decided to convey the themes and messages associated with and conveyed through the essay in the form of a website.

This website which is a multimodal version of the argumentative essay "Ai should augment human intelligence, not replace it" incorporates the visual, linguistic and spatial medium to convey information in a readable and aesthetically pleasing fashion. Various links have been provided for the curious mind's further perusal on the subject. These links include YouTube videos which employ the audio-visual medium and blog posts which further utilizes the linguistic medium to convey information. The cordially inviting do not click sign acts as a gateway to the information we have collected and displayed. The Future of AI

---

The conflicting sentiments behind AI on whether it’ll result in human devastation or development has been going on since the 1920 play “Rossum’s Universal Robots” by Czech writer Karel Čapek, which introduced the word “robot” to describe artificial workers created by humans. In the play, the robots rebel against their creators and eventually take over the world. Over the years, AI has been grossly misunderstood by many and the conglomerates who understand it want to capitalize AI as a commodity only affordable by the privileged. The essay delves deeper into the subject of AI and it’s future implications examining all the nuances behind the subject. The ultimate conclusion to the age old question,”Will AI replace human beings” has been answered and the answer is a resounding no.

Why? Because what we can conclude from every development that has taken place in the field of AI thus far is that it can do “jobs”. It can work. It can complete tasks. But can it evoke emotions? Can it explore human dilemmas? Can it understand emotions in order to explain it? Can it create meaningful art? To a certain degree, it actually can but that remains irrelevant. The ultimate consumers of any product, be it a movie, music or a video game are human beings. Although an AI generated product could be enough to satiate the needs of an average human, Nothing can significantly impact a person’s emotions, perspective and mindset the way human interference can. Without human interference, AI can never venture beyond what already exists. AI may be able to work with already existing ideas and sentiments and generate matter faster than a human being can but It is the human mind that explores the unknown and brings about the existence of new ideologies and sentiments that can effectively and significantly shape the world. When sentiments and emotions are manipulated by the human mind to convey something novel, thought is provoked. And the world is meaningless without constant novelties being introduced. Without discovery, development will become stagnant and the world becomes repetitive. So human beings and AI must compliment each other in order to bring into existence a developing world.

Claim, Warrant & Evidence

Claim : AI should augment human intelligence, not replace it

Warrant : An effective and efficient use of resources for development is always appreciated.

Evidence : AI can never truly replace human beings as they are incapable of taking into consideration the complexities of morality, culture and emotions. Since AI can use pre existing knowledge and apply it to complete tasks with efficiency and accuracy, to a considerable degree, it is an asset. When human intelligence and AI are utilized in a manner in which they compliment each other, considerable progress is made which goes beyond what is achievable by humans or AI alone.
